INTRODUCTION

This is not a true-crime story about Jeffrey Epstein and his island. Every prior treatment begins at the end—with the girls, the flight logs, the famous names—and works backward, never reaching a satisfying origin. The CIA Asset & The Man Who Didn't Take The Bait starts at the true beginning: the covert intelligence and financial apparatus the United States government constructed during World War II, how it was funded outside congressional oversight for eight decades, and how an unremarkable boy from a working-class family in Brooklyn was identified, groomed, and deployed as one of its most valuable financial and blackmail operatives.

Epstein is not the story—he is the instrument through which the apparatus becomes visible.

This machine did not die in a jail cell on August 10, 2019, at the Metropolitan Correctional Center in New York City… it is still running.
